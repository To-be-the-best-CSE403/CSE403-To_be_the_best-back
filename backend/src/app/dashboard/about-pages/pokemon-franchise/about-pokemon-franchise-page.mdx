# About the Pokémon franchise

The Pokémon franchise was created in Japan in 1996 and is still developing new games and apps today. The first Pokémon games (Pokémon Red and Blue) were developed by Game Freak and published by Nintendo in 1996 for the Game Boy. Since then, the Pokémon franchise has expanded to have comics, animation series, movies, a trading card game, merchandise, more main video games installments, and spin-off games. Given Pokémon's age, both kids and adults are fans of Pokémon, with Pokémon being a part of some of these adults' childhoods.

## Pokémon Games ##
Currently, there are the 39 games in main Pokémon video game installments with four DLCs released across 27 years. The 39 games involve both original games as well as remakes of older games. Over the Pokémon franchise's lifetime, the games have experienced millions of sales where, as of March 2023, Pokémon has shipped over 480 million units of software in 9 languages.

Beyond the 39 main installments, there are other Pokémon games, game series, and mobile apps of varying genres made by different game development studios.

Some Pokémon side game series are:

Some Pokémon mobile apps are:

## Pokémon Comic/Manga Series ##
The comic/manga series for Pokémon is called [Pokémon Adventures](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Adventures) and has 16 story arcs making up 65 volumes at the moment. The story arcs are based on each set of main Pokémon game installments, for example the [Diamond & Pearl arc](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Adventures#Diamond_&_Pearl_arc_(Volume_30%E2%80%9338)) is based on Pokémon Diamond & Pearl games.

The comics were originally published in Japanese but has since been translated to English, Chinese, Finnish, French, German, Greek, Italian, Korean, Malaysian, Norwegian, Polish, Portuguese, Spanish, Thai, and Vietnamese.

## Pokémon Animation/Anime Series and Movies ##
The Pokémon animated TV series began airing in 1997 in Japan and in 1998 in the USA. There are currently 1,232 20 minute episodes in the main Pokémon series. The TV shows have aired in 192 countries and regions to date. The majority of the episodes follow a young Pokémon trainer named Ash Ketchum (in international episode) and his friends as they journey across various regions. These regions are mostly based off the locations from the main video game series. Ash Ketchum travels with the friends he encounters in order to become a Pokémon Master; in other words, the best Pokémon trainer.

In 2023, the animated series starring Ash Ketchum ended and two new protagonists for the new installations in the Pokémon TV series, Liko and Roy, were introduced. Liko and Roy's series also features them exploring various regions from the main game series.

From 1999 until now, Pokémon has released 23 animated films, 1 live action movie, and 5 special films were released both as films and separate episodes.

## Pokémon Trading Card Game (TCG) ##
The Pokémon TCG game began in 1996 following the release of the first Pokémon games and has since released over 99 expansion sets, each with their own set of cards that fans can purchase, trade, and collect. The Pokémon TCG has its own version of Pokémon battles and has its own competitive scene in the USA and in Japan. Since March 2023, the Pokémon TCG has produced over 52.9 billion cards in 14 languages and sold in 89 countries and regions.

## Pokémon Merchandise ##
The Pokémon franchise has released many products featuring its Pokémon and characters.
From toys and plushes, to apparel and home decor, the Pokémon franchise has a hugh variety of merchandise. Its products also includes: holiday decorations, figures, jewelry, stationary, pins, and video game accessories.
